Get Started with Neon Blue

From data connection to your first experiment results — here's how it works.

Section 2.1

Connecting Your Data

Before we can run experiments, we need access to your CRM platform and relevant data. Here's what the setup process looks like:

Platform access. We'll need a team member account in your CRM platform (Braze, Iterable, Klaviyo, etc.) with permissions to create and manage campaigns, canvases, and segments.

Data review. We'll review your existing user attributes, event data, and segmentation to understand what's available for targeting and personalization. This helps us design experiments that use your actual data rather than theoretical attributes.

Flow audit. We'll map your current lifecycle flows — welcome series, onboarding, upsell, reactivation, winback, etc. — to identify the highest-impact testing opportunities.

Tracking sheet setup. We'll set up a shared tracking sheet that serves as the single source of truth for all experiments: what's running, what's planned, and what we've learned.

Timeline

The onboarding process typically takes 1–2 weeks, depending on platform complexity and data availability.

Section 2.2

Launching Your First Test

Once setup is complete, here's how a test goes from idea to live:

1

We propose the experiment

Based on our flow audit and your priorities, we'll present a test proposal that includes the hypothesis, target flow, variant concepts, and expected timeline.

2

You review and approve

You'll review the proposal, give feedback on the creative direction, and approve the test. This is your checkpoint to ensure everything aligns with brand and strategy.

3

We build and launch

We create the variants, set up the experiment in your platform, configure the audience splits, and launch. We handle the technical implementation end-to-end.

4

We monitor

Once live, we monitor the experiment for delivery issues, anomalies, and early signals. You don't need to babysit the test.

Section 2.3

Seeing Results and Continuing

When an experiment reaches statistical significance (or a pre-defined time window), we analyze the results and prepare a summary — and then the program builds momentum.

Results Review

We analyze what won, what lost, what we learned, and what it means for next steps — then prepare a clear summary for your team.

Weekly Sync

We review results together in a weekly call — discussing learnings, making decisions about scaling winners, and planning the next round of tests.

Iteration

Most experiments generate new questions. A winning subject line might prompt us to test the body copy next. The program builds momentum over time.

Learning Library

Every result gets documented in your tracking sheet. Over time, this becomes a valuable knowledge base about what works for your specific audience.
